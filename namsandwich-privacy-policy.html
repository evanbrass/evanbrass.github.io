Privacy Policy — NAM Sandwich

Effective date: June 27, 2026  ·  Last updated: June 27, 2026

NAM Sandwich (“the app”) is a guitar amp-modeling app. We designed the app to be private by default: your playing and your settings stay on your device. This policy explains exactly what the app accesses, why, and what leaves your device (very little).

Short version

  • Your guitar audio is processed live on your device and is never recorded, stored, or transmitted.
  • The app has no analytics, no tracking, no advertising, and no developer-run servers.
  • The only data that leaves your device is when you choose to sign in to and download tones from Tone3000, and when Apple processes an optional subscription purchase.
  • Your captures, sandwiches (presets), and settings are stored only on your device.

What the app accesses

Microphone (guitar input)

The app needs microphone access to receive your instrument signal (typically through an audio interface) so it can run it through the amp models in real time. This audio is processed live, in memory, on your device. It is not recorded to a file, not saved, and not sent anywhere. Turning off microphone permission simply stops audio from flowing through the app.

Tone3000 (optional, only when you use it)

The app can browse and download .nam and impulse-response files from Tone3000 (tone3000.com). This happens only when you tap to sign in or download:

  • Sign-in uses Tone3000’s standard OAuth flow in a secure system web view. We do not see or store your Tone3000 password. The app stores only the access token needed to download files you request.
  • When you download a tone, the app requests that file from Tone3000 over an encrypted connection and saves it locally to your library.
  • Tone3000 is operated by a third party and has its own privacy policy and content terms, which govern your account and the files you download. Please review Tone3000’s policies for how they handle your account data.

We do not send your personal information to Tone3000 beyond what its sign-in flow requires, and we do not receive your Tone3000 account details.

Subscriptions (optional, handled by Apple)

If you purchase a subscription, the transaction is handled entirely by Apple through the App Store using your Apple ID. We do not receive or store your name, payment card, or billing details. Apple’s privacy policy governs that purchase. We only receive the anonymous subscription status Apple provides so the app can unlock paid functionality.

On-device storage

Your downloaded captures and IR files, your saved sandwiches (presets), performance slots, and app settings are stored locally on your device using Apple’s on-device storage. This data is not uploaded to us or to any third party.

Note on iCloud: This version of the app does not sync your library to iCloud. A future version may offer optional iCloud sync (stored in your private iCloud database under your Apple ID); if and when that ships, this policy will be updated to describe it.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not collect analytics or usage statistics.
  • We do not use third-party tracking, advertising, or marketing SDKs.
  • We do not operate our own servers that receive your data.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information.
  • We do not record or retain your audio.

Data retention and deletion

Because your captures, presets, and settings live on your device, you control them directly. You can delete individual captures and sandwiches in the app, and deleting the app removes all of its locally stored data. Files you downloaded from Tone3000 are stored locally and are removed when you delete them or delete the app.

Children’s privacy

The app is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The app collects no personal information from any user as described above.

Security

Network requests the app makes (to Tone3000 and Apple) use encrypted (HTTPS/TLS) connections. Locally stored data is protected by your device’s standard security and file protection.

Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles data (for example, when optional iCloud sync is added), we will update this policy and revise the “Last updated” date above.
